My Name is Emma Saiz

I’m a UX/UI designer dedicated to developing digital products rooted in a deep understanding of user needs. My experience learning Russian and working with people on the Autism Spectrum Disorder has taught me one of the core principals of good design: empathy.

The iterative and collaborative process of design makes every design “problem” a fun and interesting challenge. I enjoy projects where the context and audience are well understood, and that comes with doing research. Thorough research not only inspires empathy, but makes the entire design process more meaningful.

Unrelated to work, I love enjoying Colorado’s outdoors from hiking to skiing! You can also find me volunteering at the Dumb Friend’s League and shelving at the Denver Performing Art Center’s internal library.
